Week 5 Documentation

1 Why are the variables declared as private?

The variables are declared as private to protect the data from direct access outside the class. This is called encapsulation and helps keep the data safe.



2. What happens if the variables are declared public?

If the variables are declared public, any class can access and modify them directly. This may cause incorrect or unwanted changes to the data.


3 Why do we use getters and setters?

Getters and setters provide controlled access to private variables. They allow reading and updating data while maintaining encapsulation and improving program security.
